Best Chicken Parmesan

ATK - Andrea Geary

Yield Serves 4 Cook time 1½ hours, plus 20 minutes resting

Ingredients

Chicken:

  • 2 6- to 8-oz. boneless, skinless chicken breasts trimmed, halved horizontally, and pounded ½ inch thick
  • 1 t. kosher salt
  • 2 oz. whole-milk mozzarella cheese shredded (½ cup)
  • 2 oz. fontina cheese shredded (½ cup)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 T. all-purpose flour
  • 1½ oz. Parmesan cheese grated (¾ cup)
  • ½ C. panko bread crumbs
  • ½ t. garlic powder
  • ¼ t. dried oregano
  • ¼ t. pepper
  • ⅓ C. vegetable oil
  • ¼ C. fresh basil torn

Sauce:

  • 2 T. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves minced
  • kosher salt
  • pepper
  • ¼ t. dried oregano
  • pinch red pepper flakes
  • 1 28-oz. can crushed tomatoes
  • ¼ t. sugar
  • 2 T. fresh basil coarsely chopped

Directions

Sauce:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat until shimmering.
  2. Add garlic, ¾ teaspoon salt, oregano, and red pepper flakes; cook, stirring occasionally, until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Stir in crushed tomatoes and sugar; increase heat to high and bring to a simmer.
  4. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until thickened, about 20 minutes.
  5. Off heat, stir in basil and remaining 1 tablespoon oil; season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover and keep warm.

Chicken:

  1. Sprinkle each side of each cutlet with ⅛ teaspoon salt and let stand at room temperature for 20 minutes.
  2. Combine mozzarella and fontina in a bowl; set aside.
  3. Adjust oven rack 4 inches from broiler element and heat broiler.
  4. Whisk egg and flour together in a shallow dish until smooth.
  5. Combine Parmesan, panko, garlic powder, oregano, and pepper in a second shallow dish.
  6. Pat chicken dry with paper towels. Working with one cutlet at a time, dredge in egg mixture, allowing excess to drip off, then coat in Parmesan mixture, pressing gently so crumbs adhere. Transfer to a large plate.
  7. Heat oil in a 10-inch nonstick skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  8. Carefully place 2 cutlets in skillet and cook without moving until bottoms are deep golden brown, 1½ to 2 minutes.
  9. Flip and cook second side until deep golden brown, 1½ to 2 minutes. Transfer to paper towel–lined plate and repeat with remaining cutlets.
  10. Place cutlets on a rimmed baking sheet and sprinkle cheese mixture evenly over each cutlet.
  11. Broil until cheese is melted and beginning to brown, 2 to 4 minutes.
  12. Transfer to serving platter, top each cutlet with 2 tablespoons sauce, sprinkle with basil, and serve immediately, passing remaining sauce separately.

Notes

  • Use Tuttorosso or Muir Glen crushed tomatoes for best flavor.
  • This recipe makes enough sauce for the chicken plus four servings of pasta.
  • Serve with pasta and a simple green salad.
  • Why This Recipe Works: Salting the chicken keeps it moist, replacing some breadcrumbs with Parmesan ensures a crisp crust, and mixing mozzarella with fontina prevents rubbery cheese. The sauce is simple but flavorful.
